Dataset Overview

This dataset contains statistics for a sample of 49301 U.S. residential addresses and their sales transactions in the City of Boston between 2000-2023. The dataset contains various columns providing information related to real estate transactions. The 'address' column holds 23,769 unique residence addresses, while the 'street' column contains 492 different street names and the 'zip' column consists of 38 unique numeric zip codes. Property types are categorized under 'proptype', condensing use codes and usages into broader categories like apartment, condo, commercial, etc. Dates of transactions are recorded in the 'date' column, with 5,659 unique entries. Other notable columns include 'price', indicating the sale amount, 'yearbuilt' representing the original construction year, and 'lotsize' denoting the size of the property in square feet. Various binary columns indicate whether the buyer or seller is a business entity, a trust, a bank, or a government entity. Information regarding flips, investor types, and ownership status is also provided. Overall, the dataset offers comprehensive insights into real estate transactions, including property details, financial aspects, and participant characteristics. This dataset is provided by Metropolitan Area Planning Council (MAPC). I'm interested in this data because the various factors on housing price may allow us to gain more insights into real estate market trends, investment opportunities, and the behavior of buyers and sellers within the industry.

Discoveries & Insights

Figure 1: Overall Trend in Residential Housing Prices Over the Years

The first step is to take a look at our data set. This chart shows the average housing prices for each year between 2000 and 2023. We observe an overall increase in housing prices until 2019, with a peak in 2017 and a sudden drop in 2008 and in 2018. The drop in 2008 was likely due to the 2008 housing market crash, and the drop in 2018 was likely due to the interest rate volatility, sales being pulled forward in anticipation of higher rates in the future, and the continuous constraint in housing supply. Not enough data was collected for 2023 so the below analysis will be conducted from years 2000 to 2023.

Figure 2: Percentage Change in Housing Prices Over Time

In this graph we plot the percentage change in housing prices over time. I calculated the percentage change between consecutive years by using this formula: ([Price] - LOOKUP([Price], -1)) / ABS(LOOKUP([Price], -1)). Based on this graph we can see that from 2001 to 2022, the housing market experienced fluctuating trends, with both positive and negative percentage changes observed. The years 2004, 2010, and 2017 stand out as periods of significant growth, with percentage changes of 1.4, 0.9, and 0.8, respectively. Conversely, the years 2008 and 2020 witnessed substantial declines, with percentage changes of -0.54 and -0.32, respectively, reflecting the impact of economic downturns such as the global financial crisis of 2008 and the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020. Despite occasional downturns, the overall trend indicates a gradual increase in housing prices, with relatively stable growth observed in recent years.

Figures 3 & 4: Seasonal Changes in Housing Prices

Now that we have an idea of what the data looks like, we can answer the first question: does housing prices change based on season? Generally, housing prices exhibit fluctuating trends across quarters, with certain quarters experiencing higher prices compared to others. While the second quarter of 2017 notably stands out with a considerable spike in housing prices, reaching 4,638,715,248.00, I excluded this anomaly from the analysis due to its significant deviation from the overall trend. Excluding this spike, the analysis still demonstrates fluctuating patterns across quarters, with some quarters exhibiting higher price averages than others. For instance, the fourth quarter of 2019 recorded the highest average price of 2,250,501,456.00, followed by the third quarter of 2019 and the fourth quarter of 2021. Conversely, the first quarter of 2003 reported the lowest average price, indicating potential seasonal variations in housing market dynamics. Based on the data provided, it is evident that housing prices do exhibit variation based on the season. Across different quarters from 2000 to 2022, we observe fluctuating trends in housing prices, with certain quarters consistently reporting higher average prices compared to others. For example, the fourth quarter of 2019 recorded the highest average price, followed by the third quarter of 2019 and the fourth quarter of 2021. Conversely, the first quarter of 2003 reported the lowest average price. Therefore, it is reasonable to conclude that housing prices change based on the season, with certain quarters exhibiting higher or lower prices compared to others.

Summary

In analyzing residential housing prices from 2000 to 2023, several key insights emerged. Despite fluctuations driven by events like the 2008 housing market crash and the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, the overall trend showed a gradual increase, indicating market resilience. Seasonal variations were evident, with certain quarters consistently reporting higher average prices, highlighting the influence of timing on real estate values. Factors such as style, property type, geographical location, lot size, and property features all played significant roles in determining housing prices. For example, certain styles commanded premium prices, while properties with more bedrooms and bathrooms tended to be larger and more expensive. Additionally, the analysis of investor types revealed diverse dynamics, with smaller investors showing significant variation in pricing strategies compared to larger institutional players. While the year built showed no clear correlation with housing prices, factors such as lot size, total rooms, number of bedrooms, and internal square footage emerged as more influential determinants, suggesting that property quality and features outweigh the influence of the year built on housing prices. Overall, the comprehensive analysis provided valuable insights for both buyers and sellers, emphasizing the multifaceted nature of housing prices and the importance of considering various factors when assessing property values.
